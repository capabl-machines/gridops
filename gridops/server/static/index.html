GRIDOPS

Community Microgrid Bridge Operator

Imagine you're in charge of keeping the lights on for 100 homes in an Indian city during a brutal summer.

You have rooftop solar panels (free energy from the sun!), a big community battery (stores energy for later), an expensive diesel backup generator (costs a fortune), and a connection to the national power grid (prices change every hour).

Every hour, you make 3 decisions: How much power to buy or sell from the grid. Whether to turn on the diesel generator. And whether to ask residents to use less power for a bit.

The battery? You don't control it directly. It automatically absorbs whatever energy is left over (or fills the gap when there's not enough).

The catch
At 8 PM every evening, everyone turns on their AC. Demand hits 250 kW. But the grid connection maxes out at 200 kW. That's a 50 kW gap. Your battery can cover it — if you charged it during the day. If it's empty because you discharged it for profit? Blackout. Rs 150/kWh penalty. On a heatwave day, demand spikes to 375 kW and you'll need everything — battery, diesel, shedding — just to survive.

The grid is your safety net
